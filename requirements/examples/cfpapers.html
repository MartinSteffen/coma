Objectives & scope

Established in 1888, the WIRR'06 series of conferences aims to provide an integrated forum for research on various aspects of Improbable Research and Irreproducible Results. The conference will especially welcome novel contributions reflecting recent developments in that area, in particular the development from unlikely into unprobable and more impossible results. Areas of interest in that field include, but are not limited, to:
  • apocryph notation, carefully explained
  • formal and semi-formal formalisms
  • quantum psychology
  • perpetua mobili and their applications
  • E-commerce
  • zero-gravity software engineering

Venue & Event

The 39th CIRRR is organized by the Institute of Computer Science of the University of Kiel, and will be held in Schilksee, an internally renown seaside resort. The accompagnying programme of shared satellite workshops -a call for workshop proposals is issued separately- will in particular include a Ph.D workshop giving young researchers an international platform to present their results.

Submisssion guidelines

The CIRRR'06 conference solicits low quality papers reporting research results and/or experience reports unrelated to the topics mentioned above. All papers must be original, unpublished, and not submitted simultanously for publication elsewhere. They must not exceed in length 150 pages in llncs.sty, and submission will be electronically as postscript or PDF. For the final proceedings, contributions are to be typeset with LaTeX5 Each paper will undergo a more or less thorough process of review and the conference proceedings will be published by Ursa Minor Press.

The website for electronic paper submission will be linked in some time weeks before the submission deadline.
